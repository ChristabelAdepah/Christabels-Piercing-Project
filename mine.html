About Kakum National Park

Kakum National Park is a tropical rainforest located in the Central Region of Ghana. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the country, known for its rich biodiversity and unique canopy walkway. The park covers an area of 375 square kilometers and is home to over 40 species of mammals, including forest elephants, bongo antelopes, and Diana monkeys.

The highlight of Kakum National Park is the canopy walkway, which is suspended 30 meters above the ground and offers breathtaking views of the forest. The walkway is 350 meters long and consists of seven bridges, providing visitors with a unique perspective of the rainforest and its inhabitants. In addition to the canopy walkway, the park also offers guided tours, bird watching, and hiking trails.

Kakum National Park plays a crucial role in the conservation of Ghana's natural heritage and provides a sanctuary for many endangered species.

About Tafi Monkey Sanctuary

Tafi Monkey Sanctuary(a scared grove) is a traditional conservation area backed by statutory enforcement in co-operation with local communities. It is located in the Volta Region of Ghana.

Tafi Atome is the home of Mona and Patas monkeys.These monkeys are found in a remnant patch of forests,which has survived fire and human disturbance around the village. These monkys are regarded as gods and as such the natives do not kill them.They are protected by tradition.Similar scared groves and burial grounds protected by local people are wildspread in the region.These can furtherbe developed for small-scale tourism in the local communities to enhance rural development.

About Mount Afadja

Mount Afadjo is one the highest mountains in Ghana standing at an elevation of 885 meters (2,904 ft).The mountain is located in Agumatsa Range near the villagesof Gbledi and Liati Wote,in the Volta Region of Ghana at the border with Togo.'Afadja'is the name of the mountain whereas 'to' in the Ewe dialect means mountain therefore,it is called 'Afadjato' by the indigenes. The correct name would be 'Mount Afadja' as 'Mount Afadjato' will be a repetition of the 'Mountain'.

Mount Afadjato is one of the most visited tourist attractions in the Volta Region of Ghana welcoming thousands of visitors from around the globe annually. About a kilometer (less than a mile) to the east,is the nearby peak of Aduadu, which is higher than Afadjato,also standing at over 900 metres.

About Wli Waterfalls

The Wli Waterfalls were formed by the Agumatsa River,which flows through the Agumatsa Wildlife Sanctuary.Over time, the river eroded the rock formation ,creating a beautiful cascade of Waterfalls.The Wli Waterfalls hold cultural and spiritual significance for the people of the surrounding villages.The Ewe people,who are the predominant ethnic group in the area,consider the falls scared and believe that they are inhabited by spirits.

The Wli Waterfalls started gaining popularity as a tourist destination in the late 20th century.Effort were made to improve accessibility and infrastructure to attract visitors. Trail were created to facilitate hiking,and amenities such as picnic areas and restrooms were built.
